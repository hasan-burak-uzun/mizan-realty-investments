Foreign Investor Considerations

Beyond state-level costs, international buyers face additional US federal requirements regardless of which state they invest in.

FIRPTA Withholding

When you sell, 15% of the sale price is withheld by the IRS. This is a credit toward your tax liability, not an extra tax — but plan your cash flow accordingly.

LLC Structure

Most foreign investors hold US property through an LLC for liability protection and tax efficiency. Setup costs $500–$2,000 depending on the state. Wyoming and Delaware LLCs are popular choices.

ITIN Requirement

You need an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN) to file US taxes. Mizan Realty can connect you with the right CPA to obtain one before closing.

Foreign National Mortgage

Non-US residents can get mortgages, but typically need 25–40% down payment. Rates are 0.5–1.5% higher than resident rates. Our mortgage specialist handles this.

Wire Transfer & Escrow

Large international wire transfers trigger FinCEN reporting. Plan for 3–5 business days for wire settlement. Having a US bank account simplifies ongoing management.

Tax Treaty Benefits

The US has tax treaties with Turkey and many other countries that may reduce withholding rates on rental income. A cross-border tax specialist can identify your savings.
